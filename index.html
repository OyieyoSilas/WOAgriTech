EU Deforestation Regulation

What is EUDR and Why Does It Affect Kenyan Exporters?

The EU Deforestation Regulation (EU 2023/1115) — effective since June 2023 — requires that all products placed on the EU market are produced on land that has not been subject to deforestation or forest degradation after December 31, 2020.

This affects 7 key commodity groups: cattle, cocoa, coffee, palm oil, soya, wood, and rubber — plus derived products. Kenyan exporters who fail to meet these requirements risk shipment rejection, financial penalties, and permanent market bans from the EU.

  • Geo-location data — exact GPS coordinates of the land where products were grown
  • Due diligence statement — submitted through the EU TRACES NT portal
  • Supply chain traceability — from farm to final product
  • Risk assessment — mitigation measures if risk of non-compliance is detected
