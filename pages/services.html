Food Safety & Pesticide Compliance

Beyond EUDR, Kenyan exports face strict EU food safety regulations — Regulation (EC) 178/2002, Maximum Residue Levels (MRL) under Regulation (EC) 396/2005, and phytosanitary requirements. Violation of any of these leads to RASFF alerts and port rejection.

  • EU Maximum Residue Level (MRL) pre-screening
  • Phytosanitary certificate support (KEPHIS coordination)
  • HACCP food safety management system setup
  • EU RASFF alert monitoring and response support

The EU Deforestation Regulation (EU 2023/1115) entered into force in June 2023. It requires companies placing specific commodities (coffee, cocoa, cattle, palm oil, soya, wood, rubber and derived products) on the EU market to prove those products were not produced on deforested land after December 31, 2020. Large operators had to comply by December 30, 2024; SMEs by June 30, 2025.
