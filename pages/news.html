Kenya Listed as "Standard Risk" Country Under EUDR Benchmarking — What This Means for Exporters

The European Commission has published its first country risk benchmarking assessment under the EU Deforestation Regulation. Kenya has been classified as a "standard risk" country — meaning EU importers of Kenyan products must conduct standard due diligence, which includes geo-location data and a due diligence statement, but are not subject to the enhanced scrutiny applied to "high risk" countries.

This is positive news for Kenya's exporters, but it does not eliminate compliance obligations. Standard risk still requires full documentation. AgriEU Consult has prepared a full breakdown of what this means for coffee, avocado, soya, cocoa, cattle, and wood exporters.
