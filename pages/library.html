EU Deforestation Regulation — Complete Overview

The EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR), officially Regulation (EU) 2023/1115, entered into force on 29 June 2023. It prohibits companies from placing or making available on the EU market, or exporting from the EU, specific commodities and products that were produced on deforested or forest-degraded land.

The key cut-off date is 31 December 2020 — all production must have occurred on land that was not deforested or degraded after this date. This applies whether the land is in Kenya, any other supplying country, or even within the EU.

Operators placing products on the EU market must exercise due diligence — collecting relevant information, assessing risks, and taking measures to mitigate them — before placing products on the market or exporting them.

⚠ Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • Fines: up to 4% of operator's EU annual turnover
  • Temporary exclusion from EU procurement contracts
  • Confiscation of products and revenues
  • Mandatory corrective measures

☕ Coffee — EUDR Compliance Requirements

Coffee is one of Kenya's most valuable exports, with over 67% going to EU markets (Germany, Italy, Finland, UK via Rotterdam). Under EUDR, all coffee and coffee-derived products (roasted, ground, instant) are in scope.

Specific Requirements for Coffee

  • GPS polygon data for each coffee plot (at least 4 corner points)
  • Evidence that the plot was not carved from forest after 31 Dec 2020
  • Country and region of production (county-level in Kenya)
  • Mass balance or segregation chain-of-custody for processed coffee
  • Internal risk assessment filed in the EU TRACES NT system
  • Due diligence statement reference number before shipment

🫖 Tea — Regulatory Requirements

Tea is not currently listed as a direct EUDR commodity — but Kenyan tea exports face other EU regulations: Regulation (EC) 396/2005 on MRLs is strictly enforced, and several Kenya tea shipments have triggered RASFF alerts for pesticide violations (ethephon, carbendazim). Our team monitors EU border rejection data in real time.

⚠ Tea RASFF Alerts — Kenya 2023–2024

22 RASFF notifications for Kenyan tea in 2023–2024, primarily for pesticide residues exceeding EU MRLs. Most common violations: ethephon, carbendazim, imidacloprid.

🥩 Meat & Livestock — Dual Regulation

Kenyan meat exporters face two simultaneous regulatory challenges: EUDR (for cattle grazing land) and EU veterinary health standards (Regulation (EC) 853/2004). Both must be satisfied for any beef or livestock product entering the EU.

EUDR Requirements (Cattle Land)

  • Proof that grazing land was not converted from forest after 31 Dec 2020
  • GPS boundaries of all grazing plots
  • Herd movement records and farm identification numbers

Veterinary Standards (Food Safety)

  • EU-approved slaughterhouse certification required
  • Export health certificate (EHC) from Kenya Veterinary Authority
  • Residue monitoring plan for veterinary drugs
  • Cold chain documentation throughout transport
